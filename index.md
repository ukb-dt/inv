# C
The delightful twist in this new pentad is that you’re no longer mapping the cosmos; you’re mapping *intelligences*—not in the mystical sense, but in the very literal, computational ecosystems that now bloom around us like strange orchids.

Patterns (World AI)
This is the atmosphere layer. It breathes correlations the way forests breathe carbon. A world-model AI doesn’t *decide*; it notices. It drifts across datasets until it senses the pressure ridges—regularities that behave like weather fronts. The difference added here is scale: these patterns are not local but planetary, stitched from countless fragments. They give you the raw, shimmering backdrop.

Algorithms (Perception AI)
Where Patterns are the clouds, Algorithms are the eyes. Perception AI slices the world into discriminable shapes. Edges, textures, gestures, the tiny tremor in a clinician’s hand as they hold a stethoscope. It adds the difference of attention. Structure. The local geometry inside the global haze.

Recommendations (Agentic AI)
Once attention has carved the world into pieces, agency starts picking paths through it. Agentic AI is a creature of suggestions. Its recommendations are little acts of will—proto-intentions. This layer adds preference. Teleology-lite. Algorithms describe a world; recommendations propose a move within that world.

Boundaries (Generative AI)
Generative systems add the exhilarating danger: they redraw the edge of the possible. A boundary is not a fence; it is a gradient between what exists and what might. This layer adds creativity, disruption, the ability to step beyond the training distribution and sketch a new continent. Where agentic systems choose, generative systems *invent* options.

New Market Pools (Embodied AI)
Embodiment is where it gets weirdly biological again. Robots, drones, vehicles, production-lines—anything with motors instead of metaphors. Once intelligence touches matter, markets stop being abstract flows and turn into literal new geographies: delivery corridors, robotic agriculture, micro-factories, autonomous transport routes. This layer adds friction, mass, cost, risk, spatiality. A new pool emerges when an embodied system alters what’s feasible to grow, move, supply, or monetize.

What difference does your new map introduce?
The system is no longer just a metaphysics of meaning; it’s an ecology of technological *becoming*. Each stage adds a mode of difference—pattern, structure, intention, imagination, and finally material consequence. This is AI not as a single monolith but as a five-part organism, each layer offering a distinct evolutionary pressure on the worlds we inhabit and the worlds we can build next.
